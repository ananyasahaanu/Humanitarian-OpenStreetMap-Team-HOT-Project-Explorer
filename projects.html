About Our Projects

Our projects aim to provide accurate geographic information to support disaster response, public health planning, and sustainable development. Each project involves volunteers from around the globe contributing data, tracing maps, and validating information on OpenStreetMap and similar platforms.

Through collaboration, technology, and dedication, our projects ensure that communities, aid agencies, and governments have access to the critical data they need to make informed decisions during crises.

Highlighted Projects

Haiti Earthquake Response

After the 2010 Haiti earthquake, volunteers mapped roads, buildings, and key facilities to support humanitarian response. This data helped rescue teams reach affected communities faster and more efficiently.

Flood Risk Mapping in Bangladesh

Through satellite imagery and local input, our team mapped flood-prone regions, helping local authorities and NGOs plan mitigation efforts and disaster preparedness programs.

Health Facilities Mapping in Africa

Identifying hospitals, clinics, and vaccination centers in remote regions improves emergency response and health planning. Volunteers contributed over 10,000 building traces across several countries.

Community Development Projects

Mapping schools, water sources, and roads in underserved areas supports sustainable development initiatives, education, and accessibility for local communities.

COVID-19 Response Mapping

During the pandemic, volunteers mapped hospitals, testing centers, and quarantine facilities to aid public health responses and improve accessibility for those in need.
